Figmint — Accessibility

Figmint is built to work for everyone. Accessibility isn't an afterthought — it's tested as part of every release. Updated June 17, 2026.

What Figmint supports

  • VoiceOver. Every control is labelled and operable with VoiceOver, including custom ones — the color wheel is an adjustable control you can swipe to change, and idea cards, web nodes, and tools expose actions (connect, recolor, move, open) through the rotor.
  • Dynamic Type. Text scales with your preferred reading size, all the way up to the largest accessibility sizes, without clipping.
  • Legible color. Text on Figmint's colored cards, tags, and badges meets the WCAG AA contrast ratio (4.5:1) across the whole color wheel — verified automatically on every build.
  • Differentiate Without Color. Anything shown with color is also marked another way (a checkmark, a border, or an icon), so meaning never depends on color alone.
  • Increase Contrast. Cards gain a defined edge so they stay distinct.
  • Reduce Transparency. Translucent surfaces become solid for easier reading.
  • Reduce Motion. Animations are minimized or removed when this is on.
  • Dark Interface. Figmint follows your light or dark appearance system-wide.
  • Larger touch targets. Buttons and controls meet the 44×44 pt minimum.
  • Nine languages. The interface is fully localized in English, Spanish, French, German, Italian, Portuguese (Brazil), Japanese, Korean, Simplified Chinese, and Russian.
  • Private by design. Everything — including the on-device AI — runs locally on your device, so using Figmint never requires being online or sharing your ideas.
